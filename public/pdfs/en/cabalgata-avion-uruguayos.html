URUGUAYANS' PLANE HORSEBACK RIDE

EXPEDITION TO THE PLACE OF THE MIRACLE

The Andes accident of 1972 not only tells us a story of Survival, but also leaves us with a deep reflection on resilience, cooperation, leadership and the importance of human values in extreme situations to apply them in our daily lives.

HISTORY

Flight 571 of the Uruguayan Air Force crashed in the Andes Mountains on October 13, 1972.

On board were 45 people: players from the Old Christians Club rugby team from Montevideo, along with family, friends and crew members.

16 were the survivors who spent 72 days in extreme conditions, until they were rescued.

DAY 1

The team awaits you in Los Molles, at El Montañés grill after 6 PM. The guides who will accompany you on this experience will receive and introduce themselves. A detailed talk about the route will be offered, followed by the "Customs", where we will select all the necessary elements for the journey that we will carry in the two saddlebags. The rest of your belongings and luggage will be safeguarded at the facilities. Dinner and overnight at the hostel.

DAY 2

Breakfast and departure from Club Andino Pehuenche Hostel to Puesto Araya. Lunch with cold packed meals. With the help of guides and local experts, we will select the horses for each passenger and begin the horseback ride. Upon reaching the overnight site, "Three Lagoons", at 2,800 meters above sea level, near the confluence of Barroso and Lágrimas streams, we will set up tents and prepare the "beds" with tarps, part of the saddle, sleeping bag and blankets. Additionally, the guide's assistant will prepare the fire to cook a delicious barbecue. The night will be under the stars, by the fire, with guitar and the silence of the Andes.

DAY 3

Breakfast. Departure to the memorial cross. Arrival at 2:00 PM, reaching a height of 3,700 meters. Tour of the place and cold lunch. Return to Three Lagoons camp for snack, dinner and overnight.

DAY 4

Breakfast. Camp breakdown. Transfer to Club Andino Pehuenche Hostel for a restorative shower and farewell dinner.

DAY 5

Breakfast. End of our services.

EXPEDITION EQUIPMENT

  • High mountain thermal clothing
  • Sleeping bag suitable for -5º to -10º.
  • Hat with chin strap (these are the straps to tie the hat to the neck so it doesn't blow away in the wind).
  • Rain cape.
  • Two changes of clothes. We recommend field pants or loose pants. It's better to travel with shirts rather than round-neck t-shirts, the former protect us more from the sun. It's convenient to carry several thin coats instead of one very thick one. Fleece coats are very suitable, ideal for any season.
  • A warm t-shirt, leggings or long underwear.
  • Warm jacket and/or poncho.
  • Wool hat.
  • Thermal socks, long socks or gaiters for riding.
  • Neck scarf, scarf and gardening gloves.
  • Swimsuit and shorts.
  • Sneakers and espadrilles. For riding, very wide footwear like boots is not convenient, they can get trapped in the stirrup. Those who are used to it can use riding boots. Sneakers should be waterproof.
  • Personal medications.
  • Insect repellent.
  • No vaccines are mandatory in the area. However, as for any outdoor activity, we suggest getting a tetanus vaccine.
  • Personal hygiene items, 2 towels (one for the horseback ride, another for the hostel). Sunscreen and lip protection.
  • Sunglasses with straps. We suggest not wearing contact lenses, as wind could cause eye irritation. A good option is personal prescription glasses with sun protection lenses.
  • Miner's flashlight, flashlight that attaches to the head with a headband.
  • Mug, deep plate and cutlery. Canteen or water bottle. Waste bags.
  • Camera. Cell phones with cameras can be used as long as they have battery.
  • IMPORTANT: We provide saddlebags.
  • No need to buy or bring travel backpacks.
