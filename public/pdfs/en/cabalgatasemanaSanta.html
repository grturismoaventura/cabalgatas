📅 Horseback Ride Program

Day 1 - Holy Thursday

We receive you at El Montañés from 11 am to 1 pm. Talk and presentation of guides. Preparation of saddlebags. Lunch.

3:00 PM: Departure of the horseback ride from Portezuelo Ancho. We ride to Valle Hermoso (3 hrs / 3.5 hours of riding).

After mid-afternoon, we prepare the "beds" with tarps, part of the saddle, sleeping bag and blankets. At the same time, the guide's assistant prepares the fire for cooking. The night will be under the stars, bonfire and guitar, and the silence of the Andes.

Day 2 - Good Friday

8:00 AM: Breakfast, we saddle the horses. We tour the great valley of Las Vegas Negras.

Lunch is a cold meal.

In the afternoon we will reach 3000 meters above sea level. "Viewpoint of the Three Valleys" a paradisiacal place. We will be able to observe the Río Cobre Valley, Matansilla Valley and Valle Hermoso.

We return to the camp where they are waiting for us with some delicious fried cakes to accompany with a good mate. We will spend the second night in the field. Dinner.

Day 3 - Saturday

8:00 AM: Breakfast. We will continue enjoying the beautiful valley that surrounds us, we will cross the Río Tordillo and pass near the Valle Hermoso Lagoon.

Lunch.

We leave for Río Cobre where we can enjoy a thermal bath in the middle of the mountains.

We return to the base camp. Mid-afternoon. Dinner.

Day 4 - Sunday

8:00 AM: Breakfast. Last saddling of the horses and mules.

Already returning, we head to Portezuelo Ancho as our final destination, where a delicious lunch and the vehicles are waiting for us to take us back to Refugio Pehuenche in Los Molles.

Refreshing bath. Mid-afternoon. Dinner and farewell.

Riding times may vary according to the guides' decisions. The program may change depending on weather and river conditions.

🎒 REQUIRED EQUIPMENT

    - Sleeping bag suitable for temperatures between -5°C and -10°C

    - Hat with chin strap (straps to tie under the neck)

    - Rain poncho

    - Two changes of clothes (gaucho pants or loose-fitting trousers)

    - Shirts (better than round-neck T-shirts)

    - Several light warm layers (better than one heavy garment)

    - Fleece clothing (ideal for any season)

    - Warm undershirt, leggings or thermal long underwear

    - Warm jacket and/or poncho

    - Wool hat

    - Thermal socks, long socks or gaiters for riding

    - Neckerchief, scarf and gardening gloves

    - Swimsuit and shorts

    - Sneakers (flat soles) and espadrilles. For riding, very wide footwear such as combat boots is not recommended, as it may get caught in the stirrup. Experienced riders may wear riding boots.

    - Personal medication

    - Insect repellent

    - Personal hygiene items, 2 towels (one for the horseback ride, one for the hostel)

    - Sunscreen and lip balm

    - Sunglasses with retainers. We suggest not wearing contact lenses, as the wind may cause eye irritation. A good option is your prescription glasses with sun-protective lenses.

    - Headlamp

    - Mug, bowl and cutlery

    - Canteen or water bottle. Carabiner

    - Trash bags

    - Camera. Mobile phones with cameras can be used as long as they have battery charge.

IMPORTANT: We provide saddlebags. There is no need to buy or bring travel backpacks.

Tetanus vaccination recommended.
